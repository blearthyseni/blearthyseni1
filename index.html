Learn about Islam!

Islam is a monotheistic faith, centered on the belief in a single God, Allah. The primary text of Islam is the Quran, which contains moral, legal, and spiritual guidance for believers. The Five Pillars of Islam include faith, worship, charity, fasting, and the pilgrimage to Mecca. Devotion to Prophet Muhammad is fundamental for Muslim believers, following his example and teachings for a righteous and dedicated life to Allah.

Hajj & Ummrah

Hajj is a mandatory pilgrimage in Islam, required once in a lifetime, performed during specific days of Dhu al-Hijjah, and includes various rites symbolizing the actions of Prophet Ibrahim. Umrah, while recommended, is non-mandatory and can be undertaken at any time, lacking the specific days and additional rites of Hajj. Both involve rituals like Tawaf and Sa'i, fostering spiritual unity among Muslims. The key distinctions lie in the obligation, timing, and complexity of the pilgrimages.
